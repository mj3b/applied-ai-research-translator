## Why v1.1 Exists

v1.0 established the working artifact chain. v1.1 explains why the artifact chain matters.

Applied AI research now enters operational settings through papers, preprints, PDFs, technical reports, online publications, vendor claims, benchmark results, and repositories. Those sources can inform governance. They cannot authorize action by themselves.

v1.1 documents the control layer between research and decision:

```text
source
  ↓
claim
  ↓
task
  ↓
evaluation
  ↓
run or review
  ↓
human gate
  ↓
decision summary
```

This is the release where the repository becomes legible as a research artifact, not only as a codebase.

## What v1.1 Does Not Claim

| Not Claimed | Reason |
|---|---|
| Production deployment readiness | The repository remains research software |
| Legal or regulatory compliance | Compliance requires independent legal and institutional review |
| ISO/IEC 42001 certification | The repository may support evidence, not certification |
| EU AI Act conformity | The repository may support documentation, not legal classification or conformity assessment |
| NIST AI RMF implementation | The repository maps to selected functions, not enterprise-wide risk management |
| General empirical validation | Pack corpus demonstrates method, not broad validation |
| Autonomous agent governance coverage | The current model is deliberately non-agentic |

v1.1 improves scholarly and governance legibility. It does not expand authority beyond the evidence.
